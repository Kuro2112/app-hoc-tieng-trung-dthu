Coach Analysis
Your Tone 3 is frequently pronounced too flat. The key feature of Tone 3 is the dip — your pitch should fall to a low point before rising again. Try exaggerating the dip in practice until it feels natural.
You often confuse zh and z sounds. Remember: zh is retroflex — touch the tip of your tongue to the roof of your mouth (behind the teeth), while z is alveolar — tongue tip touches the teeth directly.
Your fluency is improving! You're connecting syllables more smoothly. Focus now on the tonal transitions between adjacent syllables — the pitch changes need to be more pronounced.
